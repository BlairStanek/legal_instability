 In support of the Government’s stance, reliance was placed on 18 U.S.C. § 3553(b), which states that sentencing courts generally must sentence within the guidelines unless they identify a factor that has not been taken into account by the Sentencing Commission. Cases and discussions referencing the intentions behind repeating or recidivist behavior under 18 U.S.C. § 924(c)(1) were also raised to argue that such mandatory enhancements were never intended to apply identically to individuals without prior firearm convictions.
